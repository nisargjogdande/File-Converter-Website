SEO Tips for Better Results

XML Sitemap Best Practices

  • Keep your sitemap under 50,000 URLs and 50MB uncompressed
  • Include only canonical versions of your pages
  • Update your sitemap regularly when you add new content
  • Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools

Frequently Asked Questions

An XML sitemap is a file that lists all important pages of your website in XML format, helping search engines discover and understand your site structure. It's particularly useful for large websites, new websites, or sites with complex navigation.

You should update your sitemap whenever you add or remove significant content from your website. For most websites, updating the sitemap weekly or monthly is sufficient. Blogs or news sites with frequent updates might need more frequent sitemap updates.

While a sitemap isn't strictly required for SEO, it's highly recommended. A sitemap helps search engines discover all your important pages, especially if your site has poor internal linking, is new, or has a large number of pages. It can significantly improve your site's indexing.

Your sitemap.xml file should be placed in the root directory of your website (e.g., https://yourwebsite.com/sitemap.xml). This is the standard location where search engines will look for it. You can also specify its location in your robots.txt file.
